Rush Alpha Kappa Psi
Why rush AKPsi?

Alpha Kappa Psi provides a community of brothers and a professional network that offer support and guidance for career development during college and as alumni. Our selective recruitment process takes place at the beginning of each semester. To learn more about our organization, please refer to our resources on Alpha Kappa Psi and the Alpha Lambda Chapter.

What is the recruitment process?

Our recruitment process involves a series of events over several events for rushees to come and meet the brotherhood. This take place in a relaxed setting where rushees have the opportunity to learn more about the chapter, get a taste of our events, and get a feel for the brotherhood. Rush generally includes two information sessions, one Ice Cream Social, one Speed Dating event, and two closed events.

Qualifications for Membership

In order to be eligible to participate in the pledge process of Alpha Kappa Psi, a student must meet the following requirements:

  • Is an undergraduate student at Washington University in good standing with the school (open to all majors)
  • Attends at least one open rush event
  • Displays a genuine interest in business
  • Demonstrates ethical and professional character
  • Embodies the ideals of brotherhood, integrity, unity, knowledge and service
Frequently Asked Questions
When can I rush AKPsi?
Washington University allows students to rush professional fraternities as early as first semester freshman year. A majority of our recruitment comes from the freshman and sophomore classes.
What happens during the pledging process?
After receiving a bid at the end of the recruitment process, a pledge is given the opportunity to learn about the fraternity and become a member of the brotherhood by the end of the semester.
What is the time commitment required to be a part of AKPsi?
As a pledge, you are required to attend weekly pledge class meetings. As a brother, you are required to attend in Chapter Meetings. Both pledges and brothers are asked to participate in various professional, philanthropic and brotherhood events that take place throughout the semester.
Am I allowed to participate in Greek Life?
AKPsi includes brothers who are members of social sororities and fraternities, as well as brothers who do no participate in Greek Life. Regardless of your other commitments, whether they are in Greek Life or not, AKPsi does have a time commitment that is important to consider in your college career.
